## Set 1

<details>
<summary><strong>1. What is the capital of Spain?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** Madrid

**Tip:** Madrid sits almost exactly at the geographic centre of Spain, on a high plateau at around 650 metres — making it the highest capital city in the EU. The **Prado Museum** holds one of the world's finest art collections, including major works by Goya, Velázquez, and El Greco.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>2. Who is the famous Spanish artist known for Cubism and the anti-war painting Guernica?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** Pablo Picasso

**Tip:** Picasso was born in Málaga, Spain, in 1881 but spent most of his career in France. *Guernica* (1937) depicts the bombing of the Basque town of Guernica during the Spanish Civil War and is widely considered one of the most powerful anti-war works in art history.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>3. Name the famous unfinished cathedral in Barcelona, designed by Antoni Gaudí.</strong></summary>

**Answer:** The Sagrada Família

**Tip:** Construction began in 1882 and is still ongoing — expected completion is around 2026–2032. Gaudí was so devoted to the project that he moved into the on-site workshop in his final years. He died in 1926 after being hit by a tram; he was buried in the Sagrada Família's crypt.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>4. In which year did Spain win the FIFA World Cup, and where was it held?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** 2010, in South Africa

**Tip:** Spain's team, known as "La Roja," played a passing style called "tiki-taka" — rapid short passes that suffocated opponents. Andrés Iniesta scored the only goal of the final against the Netherlands. It was Spain's first and only World Cup win.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>5. What Spanish dance form, known for dramatic footwork and guitar, originated in Andalusia?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** Flamenco

**Tip:** Flamenco was developed by the Romani ("Gitano") communities of Andalusia and has roots in Moorish, Jewish, and Spanish folk traditions. UNESCO added it to its Intangible Cultural Heritage list in 2010. The three core elements are *cante* (singing), *toque* (guitar playing), and *baile* (dance).
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>6. Which tiny country is nestled in the Pyrenees between Spain and France?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** Andorra

**Tip:** Andorra is one of Europe's smallest countries and has a unique government: it's a co-principality jointly ruled by two people — the President of France and the Bishop of Urgell (a Spanish bishop). It has no airport and is known as a skiing and duty-free shopping destination.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>7. Name a famous Spanish food or dish.</strong></summary>

**Answer:** Paella, tapas, jamón ibérico, churros, gazpacho, tortilla española (any one)

**Tip:** Authentic Valencian paella is made with rabbit and chicken — not just seafood. "Tapas" is a style of eating (small shared plates), not a single dish. Jamón ibérico, from free-range Iberian black pigs, is one of the most prized foods in Spanish cuisine.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>8. How many official languages does Spain have?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** Five co-official languages: Spanish/Castilian (national), Catalan, Valencian, Galician, and Basque

**Tip:** Basque (Euskara) is uniquely unrelated to any other known language in the world — linguists call it a "language isolate." Catalan is spoken by about 10 million people. Spain's linguistic diversity reflects centuries of distinct kingdoms with their own cultures.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>9. What is the Alhambra, and where is it located?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** A Moorish palace complex in Granada — Spain's greatest Islamic monument

**Tip:** Built in the 13th–14th centuries by the Nasrid rulers of the last Islamic kingdom in Spain, the Alhambra is famous for its intricate geometric tilework, carved plasterwork, and gardens. It became a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1984 and is one of Spain's most visited attractions.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>10. Who wrote Don Quixote, often called the first modern novel?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** Miguel de Cervantes

**Tip:** Published in two parts (1605 and 1615), *Don Quixote* follows an eccentric nobleman who reads so many chivalry stories he goes mad and believes he's a knight. "Tilting at windmills" — fighting imaginary enemies — comes from this book. It's the most translated book after the Bible.
</details>

## Set 2

<details>
<summary><strong>1. Who is Spain's reigning monarch?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** King Felipe VI (since 2014)

**Tip:** Felipe VI became king when his father Juan Carlos I abdicated. Juan Carlos was credited with guiding Spain's peaceful transition to democracy after Franco's dictatorship, but later faced corruption allegations. Felipe VI has worked to restore the monarchy's public standing.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>2. Which Spanish tennis player won 22 Grand Slam titles — a men's record?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** Rafael Nadal

**Tip:** Nadal is especially dominant on clay, winning the French Open (Roland Garros) a record 14 times. He's part of the "Big Three" in men's tennis alongside Federer and Djokovic. Nadal retired from professional tennis in 2024.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>3. What was the name of Spain's dictator who ruled from 1939 to 1975?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** Francisco Franco (General Franco)

**Tip:** Franco came to power after winning the Spanish Civil War (1936–39) with military support from Hitler and Mussolini. He ruled until his death in 1975, after which Spain rapidly transitioned to parliamentary democracy under King Juan Carlos I.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>4. Name the Spanish city that is the capital of Catalonia and famous for Gaudí's architecture.</strong></summary>

**Answer:** Barcelona

**Tip:** Besides the Sagrada Família, Barcelona has several other Gaudí masterpieces — Park Güell, Casa Batlló, and Casa Milà ("La Pedrera") — all UNESCO World Heritage sites. Barcelona hosted the 1992 Summer Olympics, which transformed the city's waterfront into a vibrant public space.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>5. What famous festival in Pamplona involves running through the streets with bulls?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** The Running of the Bulls (Encierro), part of the Festival of San Fermín

**Tip:** Held every July 6–14, participants run through Pamplona's old town streets ahead of bulls charging toward the bullring. Ernest Hemingway's 1926 novel *The Sun Also Rises* made the festival internationally famous.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>6. Which Formula 1 driver from Spain won the World Championship in 2005 and 2006?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** Fernando Alonso

**Tip:** Alonso was the first Spaniard to win the F1 World Championship, becoming the youngest champion at the time (age 24). He continued racing into his 40s with Aston Martin, winning races and becoming F1's oldest race winner.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>7. What Goya painting depicts French soldiers executing Spanish civilians in 1808?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** *The Third of May 1808*

**Tip:** Painted in 1814, it depicts executions during the Peninsular War against Napoleon's occupation. Art historians consider it one of the first modern paintings and a major influence on later anti-war art, including Picasso's *Guernica*.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>8. What Spanish New Year's Eve tradition involves eating 12 grapes at midnight?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** Las Doce Uvas de la Suerte (The Twelve Lucky Grapes)

**Tip:** At midnight, people eat one grape for each stroke of the clock — the 12 grapes represent good luck for each month of the year. The tradition started in 1909, reportedly when grape growers had a surplus. Eating all 12 in time (it's harder than it sounds) is supposed to guarantee good luck.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>9. Which Spanish city in the south was the cultural capital of Islamic Spain, home to a great Mosque?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** Córdoba

**Tip:** At its peak in the 10th century, Córdoba was the largest city in Western Europe. The Mezquita (Great Mosque) is famous for its forest of striped arches in red and white. After the Reconquista, a cathedral was built inside it — it still stands as a unique hybrid of Islamic and Christian architecture.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>10. Name Spain's island group in the Atlantic Ocean, near the coast of Africa.</strong></summary>

**Answer:** The Canary Islands (Islas Canarias)

**Tip:** The Canary Islands (Tenerife, Gran Canaria, Lanzarote, Fuerteventura, etc.) are a Spanish autonomous community even though they're closer to Morocco than to mainland Spain. Their name comes from Latin *Insula Canaria* ("Island of Dogs"), not the canary bird — though the yellow bird was named after the islands.
</details>

## Set 3

<details>
<summary><strong>1. Which Spanish monarchs funded Columbus's 1492 voyage to the Americas?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** King Ferdinand II of Aragon and Queen Isabella I of Castile

**Tip:** Their marriage in 1469 united Spain's two most powerful kingdoms. In 1492 they completed the Reconquista (taking Granada), funded Columbus, and expelled Jews who refused to convert. That single year reshaped world history.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>2. What type of government does Spain have?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** A constitutional monarchy with a parliamentary system

**Tip:** The king is head of state but has limited political power — the Prime Minister runs day-to-day government. Spain's 1978 constitution, adopted after Franco's dictatorship, established this system and is still in force today.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>3. Name the Spanish Surrealist artist famous for painting melting clocks.</strong></summary>

**Answer:** Salvador Dalí

**Tip:** Dalí was born in Catalonia in 1904. His most famous work, *The Persistence of Memory* (1931), shows melting watches in a dreamlike landscape. He was a flamboyant public figure who cultivated his bizarre image deliberately. His museum in Figueres is one of Spain's most visited sites.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>4. What is La Liga, and which club has won it the most times?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** La Liga is Spain's top professional football league. Real Madrid has won it the most (36 times), followed by FC Barcelona (27 times).

**Tip:** Real Madrid vs FC Barcelona ("El Clásico") is one of the most watched sporting events on Earth. Both are among the richest and most valuable sports clubs in the world, with global fan bases numbering in the hundreds of millions.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>5. What is the name of Spain's cycling Grand Tour?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** La Vuelta a España (The Tour of Spain)

**Tip:** The Vuelta is the third Grand Tour after the Tour de France and Giro d'Italia. The leader wears a red jersey. It's typically the last Grand Tour of the season (August–September) and often finishes in Madrid. Spanish riders including Alberto Contador have won all three Grand Tours.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>6. Which Spanish region has long sought independence from Spain?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** Catalonia (also accept the Basque Country)

**Tip:** The 2017 Catalan independence referendum was declared illegal by Spain's government and led to a major political crisis — regional leaders were imprisoned. The Basque Country also has a strong independence movement; the armed separatist group ETA conducted attacks for decades before ceasing operations.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>7. What is gazpacho, and where does it come from?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** A cold tomato-based soup from Andalusia in southern Spain

**Tip:** Gazpacho is blended from ripe tomatoes, cucumber, peppers, onion, garlic, olive oil, and vinegar — served ice cold. It's nutritious, refreshing, and popular in summer. *Salmorejo* is a thicker, richer version from Córdoba, often topped with hard-boiled egg and jamón.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>8. In which year did Barcelona host the Summer Olympics?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** 1992

**Tip:** The 1992 Barcelona Olympics are also famous for the "Dream Team" — the first US Olympic basketball team allowed to use NBA players (Michael Jordan, Magic Johnson, Larry Bird, etc.). The Games transformed Barcelona's waterfront and became a model for using the Olympics to regenerate a city.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>9. Which expedition was the first to sail around the entire globe, beginning and ending in Spain?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** The Magellan–Elcano expedition (1519–1522)

**Tip:** Ferdinand Magellan (Portuguese, sailing for Spain) led the expedition but was killed in the Philippines in 1521. Juan Sebastián Elcano completed the circumnavigation with 18 survivors from the original 270. The voyage proved definitively that the Earth is round.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>10. How many times has Spain won the UEFA European Championship (Euros)?</strong></summary>

**Answer:** 4 times (1964, 2008, 2012, 2024)

**Tip:** No country has won the Euros more than Spain. Their 2024 win in Germany was powered by 21-year-old Lamine Yamal — the youngest player ever to score in a European Championship final. Spain's 2008–2012 era, with Xavi, Iniesta, and Xabi Alonso, is widely considered the greatest national team in football history.
</details>
