## 1. Geography & Nature

Spain is the **second-largest country in the EU** by area, occupying most of the Iberian Peninsula. The country is dominated by a high central plateau — the **Meseta Central** — ringed by mountain ranges.

Key mountain ranges: the **Pyrenees** (north, forming the border with France — home to the tiny country of Andorra), the **Sierra Nevada** (south, in Andalusia), and the **Cantabrian Mountains** (north coast). Spain's highest point on the mainland is Mulhacén in the Sierra Nevada (3,479 m), but the absolute highest point in Spanish territory is **Mount Teide** on Tenerife (3,715 m) in the Canary Islands.

Spain's island groups: the **Balearic Islands** (Mediterranean — Mallorca, Ibiza, Menorca) and the **Canary Islands** (Atlantic, off the coast of northwest Africa — Tenerife, Gran Canaria, Lanzarote, Fuerteventura).

Spain borders France and Andorra to the northeast, Portugal to the west, and the British Overseas Territory of **Gibraltar** at the southern tip. The **Strait of Gibraltar** is just 14 km wide — the narrowest point between Europe and Africa.

Major rivers: the **Ebro** (northeast, flows to the Mediterranean), the **Tagus/Tajo** (flows west through Portugal to the Atlantic), and the **Guadalquivir** (flows through Seville, navigable to the ocean).

## 2. History — The Big Turning Points

- **Roman Hispania (218 BC – 5th century AD):** Romans conquered the Iberian Peninsula and held it for centuries. Latin evolved into Spanish and Portuguese.
- **Moorish Spain — Al-Andalus (711–1492):** Muslim armies from North Africa conquered most of the peninsula in 711. For centuries, Islamic, Jewish, and Christian cultures coexisted and flourished, especially in Córdoba, Granada, and Seville. The Alhambra palace in Granada is the greatest surviving monument of this era.
- **Reconquista (722–1492):** Christian kingdoms gradually reconquered the peninsula over nearly 800 years. It ended when King Ferdinand and Queen Isabella took Granada in 1492 — the same year Columbus sailed to the Americas.
- **Spanish Empire (16th–19th centuries):** Spain built the largest empire in the world, controlling much of the Americas, parts of Africa, Asia (Philippines), and Europe. Silver from the Americas made Spain the wealthiest nation on Earth for a century.
- **Spanish Civil War (1936–1939):** General Francisco Franco's Nationalist forces defeated the elected Republican government, with help from Hitler and Mussolini. Over 500,000 people died.
- **Franco's Dictatorship (1939–1975):** Franco ruled as a military dictator until his death. Spain was largely isolated from the rest of Western Europe.
- **Return to Democracy:** After Franco's death, King Juan Carlos I guided Spain's peaceful transition to democracy. A new constitution was adopted in 1978. Spain joined the EU in 1986.

## 3. Government Today

Spain is a **constitutional monarchy** with a parliamentary system. King **Felipe VI** is the head of state (largely ceremonial — he reigns but doesn't govern). The **Prime Minister** holds executive power. Parliament (the **Cortes Generales**) is bicameral: the Congress of Deputies and the Senate.

Spain has **17 autonomous communities** — regions with significant self-government. The most prominent are **Catalonia** (northeast, Barcelona), the **Basque Country** (north), **Andalusia** (south), and the **Community of Madrid**. Both Catalonia and the Basque Country have strong independence movements.

## 4. Arts, Architecture & Culture

Spain has one of the world's richest artistic traditions:

- **Painting:** **Francisco Goya** (18th–19th century) painted dark, unsettling masterpieces including *The Third of May 1808* (depicting French executions of Spanish civilians). **Pablo Picasso** (born in Málaga) invented Cubism and painted *Guernica* (1937), the most powerful anti-war artwork of the 20th century. **Salvador Dalí** (Catalonia) is the master of Surrealism — his melting clocks in *The Persistence of Memory* are iconic.
- **Architecture:** **Antoni Gaudí** designed Barcelona's extraordinary buildings — the **Sagrada Família** (still under construction after 140+ years), **Park Güell**, **Casa Batlló**, and **Casa Milà** — all UNESCO World Heritage sites. Moorish architecture survives in the **Alhambra** (Granada) and the **Mezquita** (Mosque-Cathedral of Córdoba).
- **Music & Dance:** **Flamenco** — originating in Andalusia's Romani communities — is one of Spain's most recognisable cultural exports (UNESCO Intangible Heritage). Spain also has a rich classical guitar tradition.
- **Literature:** **Miguel de Cervantes** wrote *Don Quixote* (1605–1615), widely considered the first modern novel and one of the greatest works in world literature.
- **Film:** Director **Pedro Almodóvar** has brought Spanish cinema international acclaim, with films like *All About My Mother* and *Talk to Her*.

## 5. Food, Language & Daily Life

Spanish food varies dramatically by region. **Paella** originated in Valencia — the authentic version uses rabbit and chicken, not just seafood. **Tapas** is a style of eating (small shared plates), not a single dish. **Jamón ibérico** (cured Iberian ham from free-range black pigs) is one of Spain's most prized foods. **Gazpacho** — a cold tomato soup from Andalusia — is refreshing and nutritious. **Churros** with chocolate are a popular breakfast or snack. **Tortilla española** (potato omelette) is the nation's great everyday dish.

Spanish daily rhythms are famously different from northern Europe: lunch (the main meal) is eaten from 2–4 pm, dinner from 9–11 pm, and the **siesta** (afternoon rest) remains part of life in many regions.

Spanish is the official national language (Castilian), but Spain has five co-official languages: **Catalan** (spoken by ~10 million in Catalonia and the Balearics), **Valencian** (related to Catalan), **Galician** (close to Portuguese), and **Basque** (Euskara — uniquely unrelated to any other known language in the world).

## 6. Sports

**Football:** Spain is one of the world's great football nations. They won the **FIFA World Cup in 2010** (South Africa) and the **UEFA European Championship** in 1964, 2008, 2012, and 2024 — four Euros in total. The national team is nicknamed *La Roja* (The Reds). **Real Madrid** and **FC Barcelona** are the two most globally followed club teams; their rivalry, "El Clásico," is one of the most watched sporting events on Earth.

**Tennis:** **Rafael Nadal** won 22 Grand Slam titles — a men's record — and is especially dominant on clay, with 14 French Open titles. He retired from professional tennis in 2024. **Carlos Alcaraz** has emerged as his successor, winning multiple Grand Slams in his early twenties.

**Formula 1:** **Fernando Alonso** won the World Championship in 2005 and 2006, the first Spaniard to do so. He continued racing into his 40s with Aston Martin.

**Cycling:** Spain's **Vuelta a España** is one of the three Grand Tours (leader wears a red jersey). Spanish cyclists including Alberto Contador have won all three Grand Tours.
