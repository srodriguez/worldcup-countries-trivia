# Brasil Quiz — Answers & Study Tips (Sets 1–3)

A reference sheet for all 30 questions. Each tip gives a bit of extra background — useful for remembering the answer *and* for impressing whoever asks the follow-up question.

-----

## Set 1

**1. What is the capital of Brasil?**
Answer: Brasília
Tip: Many people guess Rio de Janeiro — but Brasília became the capital in 1960, purpose-built in the interior of the country to develop the interior regions. It was designed by Oscar Niemeyer and urban planner Lúcio Costa, and is itself a UNESCO World Heritage Site for its modernist architecture.

**2. Brasil is the largest country in South America. What is the name of the world's largest rainforest that covers much of Brasil?**
Answer: The Amazon Rainforest (Amazônia)
Tip: The Amazon covers about 5.5 million km² and contains around 10% of all species on Earth. The Amazon River is the world's largest river by water volume. Around 60% of the Amazon lies within Brasil — making Brasil's environmental policies a matter of global significance.

**3. How many times has Brasil won the FIFA World Cup?**
Answer: 5 times (1958, 1962, 1970, 1994, 2002)
Tip: No other country has won more. Brasil is also the only country to have qualified for every single FIFA World Cup since 1930. Their 1970 team, featuring Pelé, is widely considered the greatest international football team of all time.

**4. What is the official language of Brasil, and why is it different from the rest of South America?**
Answer: Portuguese — because Brasil was colonised by Portugal, not Spain
Tip: The Treaty of Tordesillas (1494) divided the "New World" between Spain and Portugal. The eastern part of South America — what became Brasil — fell to Portugal. Every other mainland South American country speaks Spanish.

**5. Which legendary Brasilian footballer, widely considered one of the greatest of all time, won three FIFA World Cups?**
Answer: Pelé (Edson Arantes do Nascimento)
Tip: Pelé won the World Cup in 1958 (aged 17 — the youngest World Cup winner ever), 1962, and 1970. He scored over 1,000 career goals and died in December 2022.

**6. What is the name of the famous carnival city in Brasil, and when does it take place?**
Answer: Rio de Janeiro; Carnival takes place in February or March (before Lent)
Tip: Rio Carnival is the world's largest carnival, attracting over 2 million people per day. Samba schools compete in the Sambadrome with elaborate floats and costumes. The parade is judged on drumming, costumes, float design, and samba dancing.

**7. In what year did Brasil become independent, and from which country?**
Answer: 1822, from Portugal
Tip: Uniquely, Brasil's independence was declared by the Portuguese king's own son, Prince Pedro, who stayed in Brasil when his father returned to Portugal. He declared "Independence or death!" and became Emperor Pedro I — a peaceful transition compared to violent revolutions elsewhere in Latin America.

**8. Brasil was the last country in the Americas to abolish slavery. In what year did this happen?**
Answer: 1888
Tip: Brasil imported more enslaved Africans than any other country in history — roughly 4–5 million people over 300 years, compared to around 400,000 to the US. This history profoundly shaped Brasilian society and culture, and its legacy of racial inequality persists today.

**9. What is the name of the famous statue of Jesus that overlooks Rio de Janeiro?**
Answer: Christ the Redeemer (Cristo Redentor)
Tip: Christ the Redeemer stands 38 metres tall on Corcovado mountain, completed in 1931. It was voted one of the New Seven Wonders of the World in 2007. Its outstretched arms and distinctive silhouette have made it one of the most recognised images in the world.

**10. Name a traditional Brasilian dish or food known worldwide.**
Answer: Examples — Feijoada, churrasco, pão de queijo, açaí, caipirinha (cocktail)
Tip: Feijoada (black bean and pork stew) is considered the national dish, with African roots. Churrasco (grilled meat) reflects Brasil's cattle-ranching culture. Açaí, now a global health food, has been an Amazon staple for centuries.

## Set 2

**1. Brasil borders almost every country in South America. Name two countries that Brasil does NOT border.**
Answer: Chile and Ecuador
Tip: Brasil is so vast it borders 10 of the 12 South American countries — only Chile (which runs down the western Andes) and Ecuador miss it. Understanding South American geography on a map is worth practising, as these border questions come up frequently.

**2. What style of football is Brasil famous for, known in Portuguese as "jogo bonito"?**
Answer: The beautiful game — creative, joyful, technically skilled football
Tip: "Jogo bonito" (the beautiful game) is a phrase associated with Pelé. It describes Brasil's traditional attacking style — flair, improvisation, samba-influenced movement — in contrast to more defensive, tactical European styles. The 1970 World Cup Brasil team is considered its greatest expression.

**3. What is the name of the Brasilian musical style that influenced jazz and became internationally famous through "The Girl from Ipanema"?**
Answer: Bossa Nova
Tip: Bossa Nova ("new wave") emerged in Rio in the late 1950s, combining samba rhythms with jazz harmonies and soft, understated singing. "The Girl from Ipanema" (written by Antônio Carlos Jobim and Vinícius de Moraes, 1962) is one of the most recorded songs in history.

**4. Which Brasilian racing driver, three-time Formula One World Champion, is considered one of the greatest drivers in history and died in a crash in 1994?**
Answer: Ayrton Senna
Tip: Senna won the F1 World Championship in 1988, 1990, and 1991. He was killed at the San Marino Grand Prix at Imola on 1 May 1994 — a national day of mourning in Brasil. He remains an iconic figure, known for his raw speed and religious faith.

**5. What is the name of the world's largest tropical wetland, located mostly in Brasil?**
Answer: The Pantanal
Tip: The Pantanal covers up to 195,000 km² across Brasil, Bolivia, and Paraguay — the world's largest tropical wetland. It's one of the world's most important biodiversity hotspots, home to jaguars, giant otters, capybaras, and hundreds of bird species.

**6. Brasil hosted two major sporting events in the 2010s. Name both.**
Answer: The 2014 FIFA World Cup and the 2016 Summer Olympics (in Rio de Janeiro)
Tip: Brasil was the first South American country to host the Olympics. Both events were controversial — Brazil spent billions on stadiums and infrastructure while facing economic recession and public protests (the "Vinegar Revolution" of 2013). Several 2014 World Cup stadiums became "white elephants" in cities without major football clubs.

**7. What is the name of Brasil's most important river for its own population, which runs entirely within Brasil?**
Answer: The São Francisco River
Tip: The São Francisco (known as "Velho Chico" — "Old Frank") is Brasil's most important river for people — it provides water and hydroelectric power to millions in the arid northeast. Unlike the Amazon (which drains to the sea without passing through major cities), the São Francisco runs through the heart of Brasil's most drought-affected region.

**8. Brasil has the largest population of Japanese descent outside Japan. Why did Japanese people migrate to Brasil in large numbers?**
Answer: From around 1908, Japanese immigrants were recruited to work on Brasilian coffee plantations after slavery was abolished and immigration from Europe began to decline; Brasil actively sought workers
Tip: The first Japanese immigrants arrived in Santos in 1908. Today, the Japanese-Brasilian community numbers around 1.5 million. São Paulo's Liberdade neighbourhood is the largest Japanese community outside Japan. This history shows how migration transformed Brasil's already-diverse population.

**9. What is the name of the waterfall system on Brasil's border with Argentina, considered one of the world's natural wonders?**
Answer: Iguaçu Falls (Cataratas do Iguaçu)
Tip: Iguaçu Falls consists of 275 individual waterfalls stretching nearly 3 km wide. At its widest point, it produces a spray visible from 20 km away. Eleanor Roosevelt reportedly exclaimed "Poor Niagara!" when she first saw it. It's a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

**10. Brasil's national football team wears yellow shirts and blue shorts. What are these colours called, and where do they come from?**
Answer: The "Canarinho" (little canary) strip; the colours come from Brasil's national flag (green and yellow representing forests and gold, blue representing the sky)
Tip: Brasil originally wore white, but after a famous defeat to Uruguay in the 1950 World Cup Final on home soil (the "Maracanazo"), they switched to yellow and blue. The canary-yellow shirt is one of the most iconic images in sport.

## Set 3

**1. What does the phrase on Brasil's national flag mean?**
Answer: "Ordem e Progresso" — Order and Progress
Tip: The flag was adopted in 1889 when the republic was declared. The green represents the forests, the yellow diamond represents gold (and Brasil's mineral wealth), and the blue globe shows the stars over Rio de Janeiro on the night of 15 November 1889 — the date of the proclamation. The motto is taken from positivist philosopher Auguste Comte.

**2. Which Brasilian city has the largest population in South America?**
Answer: São Paulo
Tip: São Paulo's metropolitan area has around 22 million people — one of the five largest cities in the world. It's Brasil's financial and business capital. Rio de Janeiro is Brasil's second-largest city and former capital, far more famous internationally but smaller. Don't confuse population size with international fame.

**3. What is capoeira?**
Answer: An Afro-Brasilian martial art that combines elements of dance, acrobatics, and music
Tip: Capoeira was developed by enslaved Africans in Brasil, who disguised the martial art as dance to practise it under the noses of slave owners. It involves fluid, circular movements accompanied by a berimbau (a one-stringed musical instrument). It's now practised worldwide and recognised by UNESCO as a cultural heritage.

**4. The 1950 FIFA World Cup was held in Brasil. Brasil needed only a draw in the final match against Uruguay to win the Cup. They lost 2–1. What is this event called in Brasilian football history?**
Answer: The Maracanazo
Tip: The match was played at the Maracanã stadium in Rio, in front of nearly 200,000 fans. Uruguay's Alcides Ghiggia scored the winning goal. It remains one of the most traumatic moments in Brasilian sporting history — the stadium fell silent. Brasil responded by changing their white home strip to yellow to move on from the memory.

**5. What is the name of Brasil's second most popular sport, in which the national teams have won multiple Olympic and World Championships?**
Answer: Volleyball
Tip: Brasil's volleyball programme (men's and women's) is among the world's best — the country has won multiple Olympic gold medals and World Championship titles. Football gets more attention globally, but volleyball has a massive following in Brasil and is often described as the "second religion."

**6. Brasil is the world's largest Catholic country by population. Name one Afro-Brasilian religion also practised in Brasil.**
Answer: Candomblé or Umbanda
Tip: Candomblé developed among enslaved Africans in Bahia. Its followers worship Orixás (deities) with African origins. Umbanda blends Candomblé, Catholicism, and Spiritism. Many Brasilians consider themselves Catholic but also participate in these traditions — a blending typical of Brasilian religious life.

**7. What made Brasília unusual as a capital city when it was built in 1960?**
Answer: It was purpose-built from scratch in an empty interior location — it had never been a city before
Tip: President Kubitschek wanted to develop Brasil's vast interior and move the capital away from the crowded coast. Architect Oscar Niemeyer designed the major government buildings in a futuristic style — white domes, swooping curves, pillars. The whole city was built in about three years and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

**8. Brasil is the world's largest producer of which two agricultural products?**
Answer: Sugar cane and coffee (also a major producer of soya and beef — accept any two of these)
Tip: Brasil has produced the most coffee in the world for over 150 years — about a third of global supply. It's also the world's largest producer and exporter of sugar cane, used both for sugar and for ethanol fuel. These agricultural exports made Brasil wealthy but also drove deforestation and, historically, slavery.

**9. What is the name of the Indigenous Brasilian sport or cultural practice that has been officially recognised internationally?**
Answer: Various answers acceptable — the Jogos dos Povos Indígenas (Indigenous Peoples' Games), or capoeira (if not already used in Q3)
Tip: Brasil has over 300 Indigenous peoples speaking more than 150 languages. The Jogos dos Povos Indígenas, held every two years, bring together tribes to compete in traditional activities including archery, canoeing, swimming, and wrestling. Brasil recognises Indigenous land rights in its 1988 constitution, though enforcement has been contested.

**10. Brasil's deforestation of the Amazon is a global concern. Name one consequence of Amazon deforestation beyond Brasil's borders.**
Answer: Examples — climate change (released carbon), disrupted rainfall patterns affecting neighbouring countries, loss of global biodiversity, disruption of the water cycle
Tip: The Amazon is sometimes called "the lungs of the Earth" — it absorbs huge amounts of CO₂ and produces 20% of the world's freshwater. Deforestation releases stored carbon (accelerating global warming), disrupts rainfall across South America (affecting agriculture as far away as Argentina), and permanently destroys biodiversity that may hold undiscovered medicines. What happens in the Amazon matters everywhere.
